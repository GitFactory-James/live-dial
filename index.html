REFDiscoveryprocess → pain → impact → priority
Core 4-step flow
  • "Talk me through it from when a job comes in to when it gets finished and invoiced. What happens first?"
  • "Out of that whole flow, what feels most manual or time-consuming day to day?"
  • "When that happens, what's the real effect — lost admin time, margin getting squeezed, jobs slipping, invoices delayed, or too much landing back on you?"
  • "How live is this for you at the moment — actively trying to tighten it up now, or more one for later?"
Pain signals — dig in when you hear these
spreadsheetspaper job cardswhiteboardtyping same thing twicequoting takes ageschasing for updatescan't track marginsall in one person's headlost visibility on floorinvoicing latestitched togetherwe're always looking at upgradingno solution is perfectthings are fluid
Three pain buckets
Money / margin
Not knowing what they're making per job. Misquoting on labour. Finding out they've lost money after it's done.
Visibility
Can't see where jobs are without walking the floor. Customers ringing for updates. Jobs getting missed.
Time / admin
Double entry. Paper job cards getting lost. Too much time on paperwork. Invoicing late.
Squeeze the pain
  • "How often does that crop up in a normal week?"
  • "Roughly what does that cost in time or mistakes?"
  • "What have you tried to fix it in the past?"
  • "Who feels that the most — you, office, guys on the floor?"
  • "If you got busier next quarter, would that hold up?"
  • "If we could sort that out, what would it mean for the business?"
If they say "we're always looking at upgrading our quoting system"
"That's exactly it — talk me through what the quoting looks like now. What are you doing it in, and where does it get messy?"

This is a live buying signal. Don't acknowledge and move on — dig straight in.
If they say "no solution is perfect"
"That's really useful to hear. What's the bit that frustrates you most day to day?"
REFQualifyaccounts → stack → headcount → fit → DM
Qualification order
  • "What are you using on the accounts side — Xero, QuickBooks, Sage, something like that?"
  • "And aside from that, what else are you using to manage things — quoting, tracking, timesheets?"
  • "How many staff have you got across the workshop and wider business?"
  • "Are you mainly fabrication with some install, or is installation a major part of the business?"
  • "Are you the person who'd normally make a call on something like this, or is there someone else?"
  • "Who else should be on the demo so no one has to relay it second-hand?"

"Just so I'm upfront — we integrate with Xero, QuickBooks and MYOB, but Sage isn't on the list yet. That matters because the accounting sync is one of the biggest parts of the platform. Without it you'd still have to run both in parallel."

If locked into Sage: "So the honest question is whether Factory still saves enough time on the ops side to outweigh that final Sage entry. Knowing that piece stays manual — would it still be worth seeing?"
